CNC Machining Course

As part of the MIT Makerworkshop, I helped organize and teach a week-long CNC machining course. Students learned an introduction to the mechanics behind feed and speed selections, how to use GWizard (a feeds and speeds selection software), and machined their very own centrifugal pumps on 3-axis ProtoTrak mills using conversatial CNC, DXF conversion to G code, and HSMWorks CAM software in SolidWorks. Instructors held lectures and milling demonstrations, designed the pump to be machined by students, and helped guide students with their machining workflow and CNC programming decisions. The pump required students to CNC machine a Delrin impeller, an aluminum impeller housing, and an aluminum baseplate. Various 3D-printed (ie. motor mount) and laser cut components were supplied by instructors.
