CNC BB8

CNC BB8 (Star Wars Droid) was a personal project I undertook to get better at CAM and CNC machining. All metallic components (aside from the main shaft) were CNC 3-axis milled, even if water jetting, or another method may have been simpler. A single degree-of-freedom pendulum, and magenetic coupling secure and balance the head. The pendulum utilizes a copper ballast to counteract the mass of the head. Laser cut acrylic flexures support a 3D-printed block with press-fit magnets. The flexures were implemented to give the head a ‘bobble’. The head contains support wheels and magnets for coupling the head to the body. The orientations of the magnets are flipped so that opposite adjacent poles serve as a high magentic shear stiffness. I ultimately ran out of time before leaving Boston to CNC the main body, so a 3D printed shell has to do for now. For its prototype nature, I commonly call this project BetaBeta8.

A demo video of BB8 rolling.


Milling of the head hemisphere. The cylindrical stock was first internally hollowed out before flipping and machining the OML.


The head subassembly. Conical Delrin wheels allow the head to roll on the body, setting the distance between the magnets. The aluminum head is machined down to between 1/8” and 1/16” to wall thickness, and a 3D printed mounting structure is used to minimize mass.


The pendulum subassembly. Copper ballast is supported by an aluminum lever. An internally pressed Delrin bushing mates the lever to the shaft. Preload to the bushing can be be adjusted via a bolt driving the displacement between the two aluminum lever halves. Acrylic flexures support a 3D printed magnet housing.


A fit test of the head and pendulum in a 3D printed body before finalizing the the body geometry.
