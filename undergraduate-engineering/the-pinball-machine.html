The Pinball Machine

The Pinball Machine is an electro-mechanical multi-level arcade game designed and built spring 2012. The player is challenged to navigated top-down through three levels filled with bumpers, kickers, pop-up targets and tunnels. Solenoids control impulsive obstacles, kickers and bumpers, which are triggered by mechanical limit switches or piezo sensors wired in reverse to detect impact vibrations. Servo motors actuate pop-up targets that raise from the floor. Flippers are fully mechanical to accomodate the small level size since sufficiently powerful solenoids do not fit in such a small game.


The Pinball Machine set up at The Cooper Union’s End of Year Show.


A close look at the finished top level. Flippers are manually actuated via a linkage system installed beneath the board. Kickers are actauted by solenoids when a ball depresses the extended lever arm of a limit switch.


The flipper linkage system converting translation motion at the location of traditional pinball buttons, to rotational motion at the flipper locations.


A photo of the lowest level which shows wire tunnels, and bent acrylic tubes that carry the balls from level to level.


An image of the game at the start of the build process.


A demonstration video of the bumpers mechanisms. Mini piezo sensors attached underneath the board to bumper bases detect impact by the pinball. An Arduino then actuates a solenoid-mounted frustum to fire the ball away.


A demonstration video of the kicker mechanisms. An incoming ball depresses the lever arm of a limit switch, which closes a circuit, actuating ball-impacting face via solenoid to kick the ball away.
